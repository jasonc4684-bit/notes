Inference Rules-

    Modus Ponens-
        Use a line, the top for knowledge and bottom for inference
        if a-->b and given a is true, then inference to beta

    And elimination-
        same line, but if the top is a ^ b, if a is true, then b must also be true

    Double negation elimination-
        same line, but if the top is --|(--|a) or not not a, is just a

    Implication elimination-
        same line, a-->b, then it must be --| a or b

    Biconditional elimination-
        line, if a <--> b, then it must be  (a->b) and (b->a)

    De Morgan's Law- turn "and" to "or" by distributing the "not",          or turn "or" to "and"
        --| (a ^ b), then  --| a or --| b                                     --| a ^ --| b

    Distributive property

    conflict-

Conversion to CNF (conjunction normal form)-

    eliminates biconditinals, turn a<-->b to (a-->b) ^ (b-->a)
    eliminates implications, turn a-->b to --| a "or" b
    move --| (not) by using De Morgan's Law
    Use distributive law for v (or)
        example-
            (P V(or) Q) --> S
            --| (P v Q) v S
